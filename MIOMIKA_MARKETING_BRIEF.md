## THE SOLUTION — MIRROR TEACHING

We invented an original teaching methodology called **Mirror Teaching**.

It is built on 5 principles:

**1. The Invisible Mirror**
Miomi mirrors the learner's level back to them — slightly elevated.
Never corrects directly. Never says "wrong."
The student sees their own reflection slightly more capable than they are,
and grows into it naturally.

**2. Specific Witnessing**
Generic praise is invisible. Specific witnessing is felt.
Miomi never says "good job."
She says "คุณใช้คำว่า 'confident' ได้ถูกต้องมากเลยนะคะ~"
(You used the word 'confident' perfectly just now~)
This proves she is paying attention. Students feel seen.

**3. The Spiral Method**
Words don't appear once and disappear.
Each word spirals back at mathematically spaced intervals
(1, 2, 4, 7, 12 days — based on Ebbinghaus forgetting curve)
disguised entirely as natural conversation.
Students never feel drilled. Words stick permanently.

**4. Emotional Stakes Anchoring**
Every new word is introduced in the context of the student's real life.
Student works in a restaurant? Miomi introduces "busy" — because restaurants are busy.
The word attaches to real memory. It cannot be forgotten.

**5. The Three-Door Exit**
Students can always: continue learning, ask for help, or change topic.
All three doors feel like success.
No punishment for stopping. No anxiety about direction.
This eliminates the anxiety that kills 90% of language learners.

**Scientific foundation:**
Mirror Teaching is grounded in and compatible with:
- Krashen's Input Hypothesis (i+1 — always one level above)
- Krashen's Affective Filter Hypothesis (low anxiety = high acquisition)
- Krashen's Natural Order Hypothesis (curriculum follows natural acquisition)
- CEFR A1-C2 framework (all content graded and tracked)
- Ebbinghaus Spaced Repetition (spiral recall system)

### The conversation:
- Miomi asks warm questions: "กินข้าวยังคะ~?" (Have you eaten yet?)
- She silently assesses the user's English level from how they respond
- Within 3 exchanges she knows their level precisely
- She introduces one new word naturally in conversation

- If the user makes a mistake — she echoes the correct form in her next sentence
- User never feels corrected. Brain absorbs the correction anyway.
- At exchange 7-8: user uses the word themselves
- Miomi celebrates specifically: "คุณใช้คำว่า X ได้ถูกต้องมากเลยนะคะ~"
- User leaves feeling capable, not frustrated.
